# Research Principles Playbook

Use this when a design tradeoff needs a grounded explanation.

## Principles To Apply

1. First impressions happen fast.
   Make the first viewport communicate product category, tone, trust, and next action without requiring scroll or explanation.

2. Familiarity and originality must be balanced.
   Preserve recognizable interaction patterns. Add originality through domain-specific visual systems, assets, content, and motion grammar.

3. Classical and expressive aesthetics both matter.
   Classical: order, clarity, alignment, readability.
   Expressive: distinct character, emotion, memorable composition.

4. Content is part of UI design.
   Weak copy makes polished visuals feel fake. Replace generic claims with concrete tasks, objects, data, and state.

5. Motion is interface evidence.
   Motion should explain change, not decorate uncertainty.

## Apply By Surface

- Landing/product site: spend expressiveness in hero/artifacts, but keep CTA and proof conventional.
- Dashboard/console: favor classical aesthetics and controlled expressive detail.
- Editor/workspace: protect canvas stability and control discoverability.
- Mobile H5/mini program: favor low complexity and short task completion.
- Game UI: favor immediate feedback and readable game state.

## Source Notes

- Lindgaard et al.: supports first-impression discipline.
- Tuch et al.: supports complexity/prototypicality balance.
- Lavie and Tractinsky: supports classical/expressive aesthetics.
- "Content or Graphics?": supports treating content quality as part of experience quality.

Use these notes to guide decisions. Do not over-cite research in normal delivery unless the user asks for rationale.
